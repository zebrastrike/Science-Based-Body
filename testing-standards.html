ANALYTICAL METHODS

Dual-Method Purity Verification

HPLC Analysis

High-Performance Liquid Chromatography (HPLC) is the gold standard for verifying peptide purity. This method separates a sample into its individual components and measures the concentration of the target compound.

  • Quantitative purity percentage per batch
  • Detection of impurities and degradation products
  • Industry-standard analytical method for peptide research materials
Mass Spectrometry

Mass Spectrometry (MS) confirms the molecular identity of each batch by measuring molecular weight. This ensures the material matches the expected compound structure — not just purity, but correct identity.

  • Confirms exact molecular weight and structure
  • Verifies compound identity independent of HPLC
  • Two methods, one conclusion — dual confirmation

PRODUCTION

How Research Peptides Are Produced

Solid-Phase Synthesis

Research peptides are manufactured through solid-phase peptide synthesis (SPPS), the industry standard method for producing high-purity peptide sequences. Amino acids are assembled one at a time on a resin support, building the peptide chain from C-terminus to N-terminus.

  • Sequential amino acid coupling on solid resin support
  • Fmoc chemistry protecting group strategy
  • Cleavage from resin and side-chain deprotection
  • Crude peptide purified via preparative HPLC
Why It Matters

The purity of a research peptide is determined by the quality of synthesis, the rigor of purification, and the thoroughness of testing. Shortcuts at any stage—cheaper reagents, skipped purification steps, or self-reported testing—result in lower purity, unknown impurities, and unreliable research outcomes.

  • Higher purity = fewer variables in research
  • Independent testing removes manufacturer bias
  • Dual-method verification catches what single tests miss
  • Full documentation enables reproducible results
DUAL-LAB VERIFICATION

Two Labs, One Standard

Lab 1: Purity & Identity

The first laboratory performs analytical chemistry testing: HPLC for quantitative purity measurement and mass spectrometry for molecular identity confirmation. These two methods together verify both what the compound is and how pure it is.

Lab 2: Microbial & Endotoxin

A separate, independent laboratory performs all microbial screening: bacterial endotoxin testing (LAL), total aerobic microbial count, and yeast/mold assessment. Using a different lab for microbial testing eliminates any conflict of interest and provides an additional layer of verification.

This dual-lab approach represents the highest standard of purity and sterility testing available for research peptides in the United States.
